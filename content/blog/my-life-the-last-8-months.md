## welcome

idk why you're here. maybe you checked my website and stopped by to take a look at the blog section to see what’s on my mind. maybe i wrote something interesting and sent it to you. i honestly don’t know, but welcome.

having a blog page is something i’ve been thinking about for years. i want to ditch social media, and honestly, most of the people i have as “friends” there feel unhealthy to me. so i’d rather share things here. you might know me, you might not, but at the end of the day, i’m just a kid trying to fulfill myself by doing what i love.

## why talk about my life in my first post ?

initially, i wanted to talk about how i set up my homelab with my mac mini when i was unemployed. then things happened. i found a job, and it’s been 2 weeks now. so it makes more sense to share the whole process as a first post.

i graduated in 08/2025 and didn’t really know what to do. my mom and my lil bro came all the way from vietnam to france to visit me after 2.5 years without seeing each other. then we went on a trip across europe (switzerland, italy, and nice). it was fun. i saw it as a reward after 2.5 years of grinding as an apprentice software developer.

i didn’t think too much at the time, but once the trip ended, everything went back to normal. i started applying. wake up at 8 am, walk my dog, go to the gym 4 days a week, apply to jobs, grind leetcode, build stuff on my own, play valorant on friday nights and weekends. that was basically my life after graduating.

it was fine at first. then the stress hit. i started feeling lost. i didn’t even know if i could make it in this industry anymore with all the AI shit going on.

## another break?

i called my mom and told her everything, that i felt lost, that i was worried. she told me to come back home, visit my grandparents, take a one-month break, and maybe do some interviews with tech companies in vietnam just to see.

it was both a good and a bad idea.

**the good part**: i met everyone i wanted to see, ate well, drank good coffee, did whatever i wanted.

**the bad part**: i actually did an interview with a big company there, and i fucked it up. couldn’t even answer a simple question like the difference between ArrayList and LinkedList. it was bad. i stayed in a shitty mood for the next 5 days.

then i celebrated new year’s eve with my family and flew back to france.

## the party ends

it had been 5 months of unemployment, and i had applied to around 200 companies. my girlfriend told me it wasn’t enough and that i needed to apply more. she was right, but i didn’t want to admit it. not because she was annoying, but because i was exhausted and tired of all that shit:

“we really love your profile, but unfortunately ...”

“we moved forward with another candidate”

or just: “since you only worked with Spring Boot/Angular during your apprenticeship, we can’t move forward because we use Nest/React”; like, duh?

i didn’t want to admit it, but i pushed harder. by mid 02/2026, i went from 200 applications to 418. around that time, i finally got in touch with 3 promising companies. two were outside paris, so i’d have to relocate. the third one was in paris, a big retail company, also the one i wanted the least.

## and guess what ?

yeah, that’s the one that accepted me.

why did i expect it the least? because it’s a middleware development position, mostly data mapping, managing data flow between services. not a typical dev role. i didn’t know shit about it, so i wasn’t expecting anything.

but they made an offer.

i thought about it for a week. here’s why i accepted:

1. it was the only offer after 418 applications and 7 months
2. my visa to find a job expires in 08/2026, so i needed stability. i don’t want to go to corporate life in vietnam
3. they said that after training on middleware, i could also work on their internal RAG system, which is actually interesting
4. big retail company = probably decent perks

## finally

i signed. i started on 23/03/2026. this is my second week.

honestly, it’s not bad. the job doesn’t seem too hard. there’s a lot to learn, but nothing impossible. whether i’ll love it or not, i don’t know yet. but the people are kind, friendly, and helpful.

that’s already enough for now.

## finally (final)

if you’re struggling to find a job, especially your first one, just know this:

- hard work does pay off.
- you’re not dumb.
- you just haven’t met the right opportunity at the right time yet.
- believe in yourself.
